FITTRACK: SYSTEM EXPLANATION & LINEAR REGRESSION GUIDE
==========================================================================

1. SYSTEM OVERVIEW
------------------
FitTrack is a comprehensive fitness management system designed to track physical 
progress and provide data-driven workout recommendations. 

The system consists of:
- A React Frontend (Vite) with a premium neon-glassmorphism UI.
- A Supabase Backend for secure authentication and real-time data storage.
- A custom-built AI Engine for progress forecasting.

2. HOW THE RECOMMENDATION SYSTEM WORKS
--------------------------------------
The system analyzes your workout history to prevent overtraining and ensure 
balanced muscle development. 

Logic:
- If your last workout was "Push" (Chest/Shoulders/Triceps), the system 
  recommends "Pull" (Back/Biceps).
- If your last workout was "Pull", it recommends "Legs".
- This ensures a standard PPL (Push/Pull/Legs) rotation, which is scientifically 
  proven for optimal recovery.

3. HOW LINEAR REGRESSION WORKS IN FITTRACK
------------------------------------------
Linear Regression is a statistical method used to model the relationship 
between two variables. In FitTrack, we use it to predict your FUTURE based 
on your PAST.

A. The Formula: y = mx + b
   - y: The value we want to predict (e.g., Future Weight).
   - x: The time factor (e.g., Days from now).
   - m: The Slope (The rate of your progress).
   - b: The Intercept (Your starting point).

B. The Algorithm (Least Squares Method):
   FitTrack calculates the "Line of Best Fit" through your data points. 
   - It calculates the slope (m) by looking at how much your weight or 
     strength changes per day.
   - It calculates the R-Squared value to tell you how "confident" the AI 
     is in its prediction. If your data is consistent, R-Squared is close to 1.

C. Practical Application:
   - Weight Tracker: If you've lost 2kg in 10 days, the AI calculates a 
     slope of -0.2kg/day. It then uses this to predict that in 30 days, 
     you will be 6kg lighter.
   - Strength Trends: It tracks your training volume (Weight x Reps) over 
     time and predicts the target volume for your next session.

4. ACTIVITY & GOAL MANAGEMENT
-----------------------------
Goals are not just static numbers. FitTrack links them to your active logs:
- Frequency Goals: Automatically tracks how many times you've hit the gym 
  in the last 7 days.
- Progress Bars: Update in real-time as you log new workouts or weights.
